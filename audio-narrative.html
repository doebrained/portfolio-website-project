Summary:

An audio narrative detailing the evolution of popular music and audio formats from roughly the late 1800s through present day. Specific formats examined are phonograph wax cylinders, piano rolls, circular records, magnetic tapes, compact disks, and audio files. Their creation, refinement, and eventual mainstream popularity or influence are examined, relating each audio format to popular music of the time as well. Ultimately, the importance of these formats to our current streaming music ecosystem is emphasized.
